SYDEOrder

This project was created at NewHacks with the help of Daniel Kim and Seth Morenos. SYDE Order is a web application that allows business owners to effectively restock their inventory by showing monthly customer purchase trends and determining an optimal restock quantity through a computer generated algorithm. The UI implementation was created using Figma, the front end using HTML and CSS, and the back end using Python and Flask.

LockOrNot

LockOrNot is a project created at StarterHacks with Jack Zhang and (Jing Liu). It is a voice activated smart lock that locks or unlocks the door when it hears your voice. This project was built using C with the integration of Google's Text-To-Speech API, tested through a Linux Virtual Machine, and constructed using an LED and breadboard.

Engineering Ambassadors

I am currently an Waterloo Engineering Ambassador representing Systems Design Engineering. Our mission is to answer the many questions of high school students regarding the overall experience in engineering at the University of Waterloo. I have volunteered at the Fall and Winter Open Houses this school year through answering questions about Systems Design (including the famous, "What even is Systems?") and giving tours of the System Deisign department on the 6th floors of E5 and E7. I am also a Shadow Day Ambassador, where high school students get matched with a Shadow Day Ambassador to experience being a Waterloo student for a day.

As a personal extension of the Waterloo Engineering Ambassadors program, I have been hosting seminars at my high school, Streetsville Secondary School, about multiple topics regarding the Waterloo Engineering application process and how to build your profile to help students stand out. Unlike many other high schools in Mississauga, Streetsville S.S. lacked many engineering based opportunites for students; there wasn't any engineering presence in terms of clubs, the robotics team was cancelled before I started high school, STEM clubs did not exist which led to no participation in external STEM competitions, there was little promotion of enrichment opportunites (such as SHAD Valley, hackathons, etc.), and there was a lack of knowledge of engineering programs as a whole. Upon noticing this, I took it upon myself to pass all the information I gathered upon entering Waterloo Engineering to the entering class of 2023/2024. I highlighted admissions timelines, properly writing supplementary applications, video interview techniques, clubs that students can start in Streetsville S.S., enrichment opportunites, starting side projects, and much more. My reason for doing this is to pass on information I wish I had when I was in high school, and to reach out to students like me and become an engineering mentor to them, something I wish I had when I was in high school.

I also offer input on AIFs. Multiple high school students have reached out to me to look over their supplementary applications. I have personally looked over more than 10 applications this year. I periodically offer advice and information through AMLOC (Applications, Materials, ) (Links, Opportunites, and C), a group of 20 000 people on Facebook that serves as a networking and professional advice for youth.
