Applying to Waterloo Engineering

I don't remember much about my Grade 12 year, except for the fact that I was a completely stressed mess, worrying about whether or not I would get accepted to several of the top-tier engineering programs in Canada.I spent my hours grinding away at school, participating in all of my extracurriculars, and browsing the uwaterloo subreddit scavenging for answers to the questions I had, all to increase my chances of getting in. My friends back then would say that I was the information broker and the one who was the most stressed about applying to university overall. But now, I'm here with a bunch of knowledge that I don't need anymore, here's my guide on how to maximize your chances on getting into Waterloo Engineering.

I would like to thank Prof Bill Anderson, Bo Peng, Paul Oh, (SYDE profiles) and the various redditors who provided me with their insight and views on the application process.

Disclaimer:

The information I have provided may be false. I bear no responsibility and have no part on your direct admissions process. This article has no affiliation with the Engineering Ambassadors, as this solely reflects my own point of view and experiences. Take all of this with a grain of salt.

Everyone viewing this article is probably at a different point in their lives. I will do my best to cater to every grade. This article is sorted in a manner where more logistical application content is near the top (catered to Grade 12s) and things that will make your application stand out is near the bottom (catered to Grades 9 - 11). This article is written on an assumption that you are attending a high school in Ontario, Canada. Use the table of contents to find out what you are looking for.
